OUR PACKAGES


GOLDEN TRIANGLE TOUR OF INDIA

The Golden Triangle Tour of India is a popular travel circuit that covers three iconic cities: Delhi, Agra, and Jaipur. In Delhi, visitors experience a mix of ancient and modern landmarks, including the Red Fort, Qutub Minar, and India Gate. Agra is renowned for theTaj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage site, along with the Agra Fort. Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, offers the majestic Amber Fort, City Palace, and the distinctive Hawa Mahal. This tour provides a comprehensive glimpse into India's rich history, architecture, and culture, making it a must-do for travelers.


GOLDEN TRIANGLE TOUR WITH AMRITSWAR

The Golden Triangle Tour with Amritsar expands the classic route to include the spiritual and historical treasures of Amritsar in Punjab. Beginning with the traditional Golden Triangle cities of Delhi, Agra, and Jaipur, where visitors explore landmarks like the Red Fort, Taj Mahal, and Amber Fort, the tour adds a significant cultural dimension with a visit to Amritsar. Here, the iconic Golden Temple, a major Sikh pilgrimage site, offers a profound spiritual experience, complemented by the Jallianwala Bagh memorial and the vibrant local markets. This enhanced itinerary combines the grandeur of the Golden Triangle with the deep cultural and spiritual heritage of Amritsar.


EXOTIC ANDAMANS WITH HAVLOK ISLAND

The Exotic Andamans with Havelock Island tour offers a pristine tropical escape in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. Starting in Port Blair, the capital, visitors explore historic sites like Cellular Jail and enjoy local markets. The tour then takes you to Havelock Island, renowned for its stunning beaches, such as Radhanagar Beach, which is celebrated for its white sands and crystal-clear waters. Activities on Havelock include snorkeling, diving, and relaxing by the serene beaches. The tour highlights the Andamans' natural beauty and tranquil environment, providing a perfect blend of adventure and relaxation in an idyllic island setting.


ASSAM AND SUNDERBANS CRUISE

The Assam and Sundarbans Cruise offers a unique journey through two of India’s most captivating natural regions. In Assam, the cruise explores the majestic Brahmaputra River, providing views of lush landscapes, tea gardens, and wildlife sanctuaries like Kaziranga National Park, famous for its one-horned rhinoceros. The journey then shifts to the Sundarbans, the world's largest mangrove forest, where travelers navigate through winding waterways and dense forest in search of the elusive Royal Bengal Tiger and other wildlife. This cruise combines natural beauty with rich wildlife experiences, offering an immersive adventure through northeastern India’s diverse ecosystems.


DARJEELING WITH GANGTOK

The Darjeeling with Gangtok tour offers a delightful blend of scenic beauty and cultural richness in the eastern Himalayas. Darjeeling, known for its stunning views of Kanchenjunga, the world’s third-highest peak, features attractions like the Tiger Hill sunrise and the historic Darjeeling Himalayan Railway. The journey then takes you to Gangtok, the vibrant capital of Sikkim, known for its monasteries like Rumtek, and serene spots such as Tsomgo Lake. Both destinations offer opportunities for adventure, such as trekking and exploring local markets, combining Himalayan charm with rich cultural experiences.


GOLDEN TRIANGLE WITH GOA

Goa, India’s smallest state, is famous for its stunning beaches, vibrant nightlife, and rich blend of Indian and Portuguese cultures. Popular beaches like Baga, Anjuna, and Palolem attract sun-seekers and water sports enthusiasts. The capital, Panaji, showcases colonial-era architecture, while the historic city of Old Goa is home to the iconic Basilica of Bom Jesus. Goa's lush spice plantations, seafood cuisine, and vibrant festivals like Carnival add to its charm. Known for both relaxation and adventure, Goa is a tropical paradise that offers a perfect beach escape.


HIMACHAL HIGHLIGHTS

Himachal Pradesh, a picturesque state in the Himalayas, is known for its breathtaking landscapes, snow-capped mountains, and serene hill stations. Popular destinations include Shimla, with its colonial charm, and Manali, offering adventure sports and scenic beauty. Dharamshala, home to the Dalai Lama, is a spiritual hub, while Kullu and Spiti Valley attract trekkers and nature lovers with their rugged beauty. The state is also famous for its apple orchards, rich cultural festivals, and traditional Himachali handicrafts. Himachal Pradesh is a perfect blend of adventure, spirituality, and natural tranquility.


SPLENDOUR OF KERALA

The Splendour of Kerala tour showcases the lush beauty and diverse experiences of "God’s Own Country." Starting in Kochi, known for its colonial history and vibrant culture, the journey takes you to the serene backwaters of Alleppey and Kumarakom, where houseboat cruises offer stunning views of palm-fringed landscapes. The misty hills of Munnar invite you to explore sprawling tea plantations, while Thekkady offers wildlife safaris in Periyar National Park. The tour concludes at the pristine beaches of Kovalam, combining nature, wildlife, and relaxation in a perfect Kerala escape.


KARNATAKA GRANVOLLES

The Karnataka GranVelles is a luxurious train journey that takes travelers through the rich cultural and historical heritage of Karnataka. Starting from Bengaluru, the tour covers iconic destinations such as the grand palaces of Mysuru, the ancient ruins of Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and the stunning rock-cut temples of Badami and Pattadakal. The journey also includes a wildlife adventure in Kabini, known for its tiger reserves and safaris. Offering comfort and elegance on board, the GranVelles experience blends Karnataka’s history, nature, and royal charm, creating an unforgettable travel experience.


ORISSA TRIBAL TOUR

The Orissa Tribal Tour offers a fascinating journey into the heart of Odisha’s indigenous cultures, allowing travelers to explore the vibrant traditions of tribes like the Saora, Bonda, and Dongria Kondh. This tour takes you to remote villages where you can witness traditional tribal crafts, dance, and customs. The tour often includes visits to tribal markets and festivals, providing insight into their unique way of life. Along the way, you may also explore Odisha’s natural beauty, from the forests of Koraput to the serene Chilika Lake. It’s an immersive experience, offering a rare glimpse into one of India’s most diverse and ancient tribal regions.


MAGICAL RAJASTHAN

The Magical Rajasthan tour offers a captivating journey through India's most colorful state, known for its royal heritage, vibrant culture, and stunning desert landscapes. Key destinations include Jaipur, the Pink City, with its grand Amber Fort and Hawa Mahal, and Udaipur, the City of Lakes, famous for its palaces and serene lakes like Lake Pichola. In Jodhpur, explore the imposing Mehrangarh Fort, while Jaisalmer enchants with its golden sands and the majestic Jaisalmer Fort. The tour also offers camel safaris in the Thar Desert and a glimpse of Rajasthan’s rich traditions, folk music, and art, making it a truly magical experience.


SRINAGAR AND LEH LADAK TOUR

Srinagar and Leh-Ladakh offer a stunning contrast between the lush valleys of Kashmir and the rugged, high-altitude landscapes of Ladakh. Srinagar, known for its beautiful Dal Lake, houseboats, and Mughal gardens, provides a tranquil retreat amidst scenic beauty. In contrast, Leh-Ladakh is famed for its dramatic mountain terrain, crystal-clear lakes like Pangong and Tso Moriri, and ancient Buddhist monasteries such as Hemis and Thiksey. The drive from Srinagar to Leh, through high passes like Zoji La, offers breathtaking views. This trip blends natural beauty, adventure, and spiritual serenity in the Himalayas.


GOLDEN TRIANGLE WITH VARANASHI

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Benares, is one of the oldest cities in the world and a major spiritual hub in India, located on the banks of the sacred Ganges River. Known for its ancient ghats like Dashashwamedh and Manikarnika, where pilgrims perform rituals and offer prayers, the city is a focal point for Hindu traditions and beliefs. The holy Kashi Vishwanath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is a key pilgrimage site. Varanasi's vibrant streets, filled with markets, temples, and traditional silk weavers, reflect its rich cultural heritage. The city’s mystical ambiance, especially during the evening Ganga Aarti, offers a profound spiritual experience.


GARHWAL KUMAYON TRIP

The Garhwal-Kumaon trip takes you through the stunning Himalayan regions of Uttarakhand, blending natural beauty, spirituality, and adventure. In Garhwal, explore the sacred towns of Haridwar and Rishikesh, known for their spiritual ambiance along the Ganges, and enjoy the hill stations of Mussoorie and Dhanaulti. In Kumaon, visit the serene lakes of Nainital and the historic town of Almora, surrounded by panoramic mountain views. The trip offers a mix of trekking, river rafting, and cultural immersion, making it an ideal Himalayan escape filled with tranquility and adventure.


LUXURIOUS TRAINS


Maharaja Express


The Maharajas' Express is a luxury train operated by the Indian Railway Catering and Tourism Corporation (IRCTC), offering an opulent travel experience across India. Launched in 2010, it has earned international acclaim for its exceptional service, often being recognized as one of the world’s leading luxury trains. The train boasts lavish accommodations, including Deluxe Cabins, Junior Suites, Suites, and a Presidential Suite, all equipped with modern amenities. It features two elegant dining cars, **Mayur Mahal** and **Rang Mahal**, serving a variety of Indian and international cuisines, as well as a bar and lounge for relaxation. The Maharajas' Express offers several itineraries that cover iconic Indian destinations, such as the Golden Triangle, Varanasi, and Udaipur, along with guided tours and cultural excursions at each stop. Passengers are treated like royalty with personalized services, making the journey an indulgent exploration of India’s rich heritage.

Palace on wheel


Palace on Wheels is a luxury train in India, renowned for offering an extravagant journey through the country's most iconic destinations, reflecting the grandeur of royal travel. Launched in 1982, it was one of the first luxury trains in India and continues to attract travelers seeking a regal experience. The train features opulent interiors inspired by the personal carriages of former Indian princes, with each coach named after a princely state of Rajasthan. Guests enjoy royal treatment in well-appointed cabins equipped with modern amenities, along with two lavish dining cars, **Maharaja** and **Maharani**, which serve a variety of Indian and international cuisines. The Palace on Wheels covers popular destinations such as Jaipur, Udaipur, Jodhpur, and Agra, allowing passengers to explore historical landmarks, forts, palaces, and wildlife sanctuaries. Its combination of luxury, heritage, and impeccable service makes it one of the most sought-after travel experiences in India.

Golden Chariot


The Golden Chariot is a luxury train in India, operated by the Karnataka State Tourism Development Corporation (KSTDC) in collaboration with the Indian Railways. Launched in 2008, it offers travelers a luxurious journey through the southern states of Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and Kerala, exploring the region’s rich cultural heritage and scenic landscapes. The train features elegantly designed coaches named after dynasties that once ruled the southern regions, with each coach providing modern amenities and royal décor. The Golden Chariot includes two fine-dining restaurants, a bar lounge, and a spa, ensuring a blend of comfort and indulgence during the trip. Its itineraries cover a range of attractions, from the historical ruins of Hampi and the palaces of Mysore to the backwaters of Kerala and ancient temples of Tamil Nadu. Known for its combination of luxury, culture, and picturesque destinations, the Golden Chariot provides a royal way to experience the grandeur of South India.

Ramayana Express


The Ramayana Express is a special tourist train operated by the Indian Railways, offering a spiritual journey based on the epic Hindu text, the Ramayana. Launched to promote religious tourism, the train takes passengers on a pilgrimage to key locations associated with Lord Rama's life. The journey typically starts in Delhi and covers significant sites in India, including Ayodhya (the birthplace of Lord Rama), Nandigram, Sitamarhi, Varanasi, Prayagraj, Hampi, Rameswaram, and other holy places related to the Ramayana. Some itineraries even extend to Sri Lanka, allowing travelers to visit important locations like Colombo and Nuwara Eliya, which are tied to the latter part of the epic. The Ramayana Express is equipped with basic but comfortable facilities, offering a unique opportunity for devotees to immerse themselves in the spiritual and cultural significance of the Ramayana, while traveling across both India and Sri Lanka.

Exploring the Mystical Sunderban National Park

Sundarban National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is located in the vast Sundarbans mangrove forest on the delta of the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers. Spread across West Bengal, India, it is renowned for being the largest tidal halophytic mangrove forest in the world. The park is a critical habitat for the majestic Bengal tiger, which has adapted to living in this unique environment. Along with the elusive tiger, the park is home to diverse wildlife, including saltwater crocodiles, spotted deer, fishing cats, and a wide variety of bird species.The Sundarbans' intricate network of rivers, creeks, and islands creates a unique ecosystem where both land and water coexist, making it a paradise for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. With its rich biodiversity, Sundarban National Park plays a crucial role in preserving the ecological balance of the region and is a vital attraction for eco-tourism in India.


Best Places to Visit in Bikaner, Rajasthan: A Guide for International Tourists

Bikaner, located in the northern Indian state of Rajasthan, is a city steeped in rich history, culture, and architectural splendor. Founded in 1488 by Rao Bika, a Rajput prince, Bikaner is known for its grand palaces, imposing forts, and vibrant desert landscapes. The city’s most famous landmark is Junagarh Fort, a massive structure known for its intricate carvings and a museum showcasing royal artifacts. Other notable attractions include Lalgarh Palace, Karni Mata Temple (famous for its revered rats), and Gajner Palace by the lake Bikaner is also renowned for its camel festival, held annually in January, which celebrates the city’s close ties to camels as "The Camel Country" of India. The festival includes camel races, folk music, and dance performances. Bikaner's desert cuisine, particularly its Bikaneri bhujia (a spicy snack), is famous across India, adding to the city's charm as a destination for cultural exploration and authentic Rajasthani experiences.


Exploring Assam’s Natural Wonders: A Guide to Its National Parks and Wildlife Sanctuaries

Assam, a northeastern state of India, is home to some of the most diverse and rich wildlife sanctuaries, showcasing the region's stunning biodiversity. These sanctuaries provide a haven for a wide variety of wildlife, including rare and endangered species. Among the most famous is Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, renowned for housing the largest population of the one-horned rhinoceros. It is also home to tigers, elephants, wild water buffaloes, and many bird species.
