PERIYAR NATIONAL PARK


ABOUT PERIYAR

AN OVERVIEW OF PERIYAR PARK

Periyar National Park, situated in the lush Western Ghats of Kerala, India, is a spectacular sanctuary renowned for its rich biodiversity and stunning natural beauty. Spanning over 925 square kilometers, the park is centered around the serene Periyar Lake, which was created by the construction of a dam in the early 20th century. The lake’s tranquil waters provide a picturesque setting and a crucial resource for the diverse wildlife that thrives in the park. This protected area is home to a range of species, including the majestic Indian elephant, the elusive Bengal tiger, and various other mammals such as sambar deer, wild boars, and langur monkeys. Birdwatchers will find the park a haven, with over 260 species of birds, including the rare great hornbill and various migratory species. The park’s varied terrain, which includes dense evergreen forests, grasslands, and hilly terrain, creates a unique habitat that supports a wide array of flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the park through guided nature walks, boat safaris on Periyar Lake, and trekking trails that offer glimpses into the park’s rich ecosystem and stunning landscapes. The cool, mist-laden mornings and lush greenery enhance the park’s allure, making it a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike. With its focus on conservation and eco-tourism, Periyar National Park provides an immersive experience in one of India’s most beautiful and ecologically significant regions.

BEST TIME TO VISIT

The best time to visit Periyar National Park is from October to March. During these months, the weather is most favorable, with temperatures ranging between 15°C and 30°C, providing comfortable conditions for outdoor activities such as wildlife safaris and nature treks. This period follows the monsoon season, so the park's trails are less muddy and more accessible. The dry weather also leads to lower water levels in Periyar Lake, making it easier to spot wildlife as animals congregate around the remaining water sources. Additionally, this season is ideal for bird watching, as migratory species add to the park’s avian diversity. The peak tourist season ensures that all facilities and services are fully operational, although it’s wise to book accommodations and tours in advance to avoid the crowds. Visiting during these months allows you to experience Periyar National Park at its most vibrant, with optimal conditions for wildlife viewing and exploration.

THINGS TO DO IN PERIYAR NATIONAL PARK


  • A must-do activity, the boat safari a peaceful way to explore the lake and its surroundings, where you can spot elephants, deer, birds, and occasionally tigers along the shoreline.

  • Several trekking trails are available within the park, ranging from short nature walks to longer treks like the Periyar Tiger Trail, where you can explore the dense forest and experience the wildlife up close.

  • A unique combination of trekking and rafting, this eco-friendly activity takes you on a bamboo raft across Periyar Lake, providing an intimate view of the park’s flora and fauna, including birds and aquatic life.

  • Guided by trained naturalists, nature walks allow visitors to explore the biodiversity of the park on foot. This is a great way to spot smaller wildlife, birds, and unique plant species.

  • An adventurous day-long trek along the park’s borders, this activity is perfect for those who want a more challenging experience and the chance to spot wildlife in the remote areas of the park.

  • Experience the thrill of walking through the forest at night with a guided patrol, offering a unique opportunity to observe nocturnal wildlife and understand forest protection efforts.

  • Enjoy a ride on an elephant or participate in an elephant bathing session, where you can interact with these gentle giants up close.

  • Visit nearby spice gardens to learn about the cultivation of spices such as cardamom, pepper, and vanilla, which are native to the region.

  • Trek to this ancient MANGALA DEVI temple within the park, offering not just a spiritual experience but also panoramic views of the surrounding forest and mountains.

  • With over 260 species of birds, Periyar National Park is a bird watcher’s paradise. Look for species like the great hornbill, kingfishers, and herons during your visit.

  • Whether on a boat safari or a jungle walk, Periyar offers endless opportunities for capturing stunning photographs of animals, birds, and the park’s scenic landscapes.

  • The park offers several eco-tourism initiatives such as the Green Walk and Clouds Walk, led by local guides, promoting sustainable tourism and conservation awareness.
